01
2020

It started with PHP.

HTML, CSS, a little JS, then PHP and WordPress. Marketing sites for clients pay the bills, and I'm good at it. But every site I built sat on top of a server somebody else managed, and that black box bothered me more than it should have.

02
2023

Then the systems classes opened the box.

CS 356 Networking. IT 220 Wireless. CS 332 Operating Systems. IT 340 System Administration. CS 331 Databases. The kind of coursework where you stop treating "the server" as a noun and start treating it as a stack of decisions someone made — decisions you can read, change, and break.

03
2025

Then the day job met the coursework.

At Industry Media, the work crossed over. Client emails breaking turned into MX record fixes. Slow sites turned into cron-job audits. Permission errors turned into actually-reading chmod output instead of guessing. The classes became the day job, in 20-minute increments.

04
2026

Now the target is cloud.

AWS Solutions Architect Associate plus CCNA before December graduation. Career target: cloud or networking infrastructure roles. Web dev was the on-ramp — a useful one, paying real money — but it isn't the destination. The destination is the layer below it.
